# Content Production Blueprint

## Product Goal

Create a strong, grade-aware cognitive learning content system for grades 2 through 6 that feels premium, engaging, and instructionally coherent.

This blueprint is designed to move content production away from isolated question-writing and toward a structured system of:

- grade-banded reading and reasoning demands
- themed content packs
- storybook and illustrated lesson flows
- reusable activity families
- stronger skill coverage across curriculum units
- better alignment between content, sessions, and mastery scoring

---

## 1. Core Content Principles

### 1.1 Content should feel authored, not generated
Students should experience sessions that feel intentionally designed around a passage, a scene, a mission, or a problem space. Content should not feel like disconnected quiz items.

### 1.2 Skills stay stable, content evolves by grade
The same skill can reappear across grades, but the content should become:
- more complex
- less obvious
- more text-dense
- more inferential
- more abstract

### 1.3 One strong passage or scene should generate multiple activities
A premium system should not write one passage for one question only. One content asset should support a family of activities.

### 1.4 Storytelling should be part of the content system
Storybook and illustrated lesson modes should be built into the content model, especially for younger and middle grades.

### 1.5 Curriculum progression should guide content production
Content should be written for curriculum units and skill priorities, not simply added to the activity bank at random.

---

## 2. Grade-Band Structure

The app is organized by grade, so content production should follow grade-banded writing standards.

## Grade Grouping Recommendation

### Grade 2
- highest visual support
- shortest text load
- most concrete topics
- clearest clues
- strongest storybook use

### Grades 3–4
- balanced text + visuals
- stronger inference and summary work
- more subtle distractors
- more nonfiction and mixed-format lessons

### Grades 5–6
- more mature presentation
- denser passages
- stronger evidence work
- deeper reasoning and logic
- more article-like illustrated reading instead of picture-book feel

## 4. Writing Standards by Grade Band

## 4.1 Grade 2 Content Standards

### Reading demands
- 20–60 words for short passages
- 1–3 sentence story pages
- familiar vocabulary
- highly visible context clues
- mostly concrete language

### Reasoning demands
- simple pattern continuation
- basic classification
- obvious cause/effect
- real-life problem solving with clear outcomes

### Attention demands
- short-response tasks
- obvious distractors
- low instruction complexity
- strong visual separation

### Best content style
- storybook scenes
- classroom and daily life topics
- simple nature and animal content
- one idea per screen

---

## 4.2 Grades 3–4 Content Standards

### Reading demands
- 60–120 words for short passages
- 2–4 paragraphs maximum for longer tasks
- richer detail density
- moderate inference
- more subtle distractors

### Reasoning demands
- multi-step cause/effect
- stronger analogy work
- category and pattern logic
- everyday decisions with evidence

### Attention demands
- stronger sequence memory
- selective attention with distractors
- moderate instruction complexity

### Best content style
- illustrated lessons
- short nonfiction
- narrative + informational mix
- guided reading missions

---

## 4.3 Grades 5–6 Content Standards

### Reading demands
- 120–220 words for core passages
- stronger evidence and evaluation
- less obvious main idea choices
- richer vocabulary and denser sentence structure

### Reasoning demands
- deeper logic
- evidence selection
- deductive conclusions
- contradiction detection
- stronger decision making

### Attention demands
- higher speed + accuracy expectations
- multi-step instructions
- more complex visual arrangements

### Best content style
- illustrated article style
- social, science, environment, community topics
- cleaner presentation with visual support
- less picture-book tone, more mature guided reading

## 6. Content Pack Model

A content pack is the main production unit.

Each content pack should include:
- one central theme
- one passage, scene, article, or mini-story
- one primary skill focus
- two to four secondary skill opportunities
- multiple activity versions from the same source material

## Recommended Pack Size
Each premium pack should contain:
- 1 story or main passage
- 1 illustrated/storybook version where appropriate
- 6 to 10 linked activities
- 1 easier variant
- 1 harder variant
- 1 diagnostic-compatible item if needed

## Why packs are better than isolated activities
- better production efficiency
- stronger session cohesion
- richer student experience
- easier quality control
- easier reuse across templates

- 35% multiple_choice
- 20% storybook_reading

- 15% tap_sequence
- 10% illustrated_clue
- 5% match_pairs

## Grades 3–4
- 30% multiple_choice
- 20% illustrated_clue

## Grades 5–6

- 5% storybook-style guided reading where thematically appropriate

## 9. Storybook and Illustrated Content Rules

## 9.1 Storybook Reading
Use especially in:
- Grade 2
- selected Grades 3–4 lessons

### Requirements
- 2 to 4 pages
- one main scene per page
- large illustration area
- 1–2 short paragraphs per page
- one question after reading sequence
- question tied directly to page clues

### Best fit skills
- main_idea
- supporting_details
- sequencing
- inference
- cause_effect

## 9.2 Illustrated Lesson
Use in all bands, but adapt tone by grade.

### Requirements
- image or visual panel
- short or medium passage
- question tied to image + text together
- supports comprehension without feeling childish

### Best fit skills
- inference
- context_clues
- compare_contrast
- evaluating_evidence
- identifying_purpose

## 11. Session Composition Rules

A session should feel unified, not randomly assembled.

## Grade 2 Session Example
- 1 storybook reading scene
- 1 main idea question
- 1 supporting detail question
- 1 sequencing or drag task
- 1 short attention task
- 1 feedback/summary moment

## Grades 3–4 Session Example
- 1 illustrated lesson
- 2 linked reading questions
- 1 sequencing / sort task
- 1 attention or logic task
- 1 session reflection or explanation

## Grades 5–6 Session Example
- 1 illustrated article or short passage
- 2 linked reading/reasoning questions
- 1 evidence/logic task
- 1 attention or multi-step challenge
- 1 summary/insight step

---

## 12. Writing Quality Standards

Every activity should be reviewed for:
- clarity of instructions
- skill alignment
- distractor quality
- grade appropriateness
- linguistic quality in Spanish
- non-repetitiveness
- meaningfulness of content

## Distractor rules
Distractors should be:
- believable
- aligned to common mistakes
- not obviously ridiculous unless the band is very early
- grade-appropriate in subtlety

## Passage rules
Passages should:
- have a real purpose
- support more than one activity when possible
- avoid filler wording
- feel like something worth reading

## 15. Production Workflow

## Step 1 — Unit Briefing
Before writing, define:
- unit goals
- skill focus
- grade band
- themes
- activity mix

## Step 2 — Passage/Scene Creation
Write:
- one core story/article/scene
- one shorter variant if needed
- visual direction notes

## Step 3 — Activity Family Writing
Create linked activities from the same source content.

## Step 4 — Review and QA
Check:
- skill alignment
- grade band fit
- distractor quality
- reusability
- narrative cohesion

## Step 5 — Seed and Test
Seed in backend and test session flow quality.

---

## 16. Diagnostic Content Guidance

Diagnostic content should be:
- shorter
- cleaner
- less narrative-heavy
- representative of the skill
- broad enough to estimate starting ability

Do not make diagnostics the most creative content in the system. The premium storytelling effort should go mainly into core sessions.

---

## 17. Priority Recommendations

## Build First
1. content pack structure
2. unit-level content briefs
3. storybook_reading support
4. illustrated_clue support
5. stronger nonfiction for grades 5–6
6. linked session families instead of isolated items

## Do Not Do First
- mass-write hundreds of disconnected multiple-choice items
- overproduce diagnostics before core session quality is strong
- treat every skill as a separate disconnected writing task

---

## 18. Success Criteria

This blueprint is working if:
- sessions feel cohesive
- students encounter varied interaction styles
- passages are reused intelligently across activities
- grade 2 content feels clearly different from grade 6 content
- the app includes storybook/illustrated reading where appropriate
- reading and reasoning content feels premium rather than generic
- curriculum units visibly shape what gets written

---

## 19. Final Recommendation

The content team should work in packs, not isolated items.

The product should support:
- stable skill structure
- grade-banded writing standards
- unit-based content planning
- storybook and illustrated modes
- reusable passage families
- linked activities per session

That is how the app becomes worth using, worth buying, and worth expanding.
